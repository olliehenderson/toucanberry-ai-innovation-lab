Making data talk back

Ask your data a question.
Get an answer.

Anyone in the firm queries structured data in plain English and gets charts, tables, and analysis back. No SQL. No waiting.

Natural language querying

Ask simple or complex questions of a Databricks data lake. Returns charts, tables, and multi-step analysis. Built and tested on a production environment.

Portfolio dashboards

KRD dashboards, investment performance views, portfolio monitoring - generated through natural language and updated live.

Data discovery

Crawls a data lake and surfaces what is available - table structures, relationships, freshness. No more relying on institutional knowledge.

Requires: a consolidated data lake with consistent naming, cataloguing, and governed access controls.
Validating what comes in

Catch bad data before it gets in.

Investment data, policy files, vendor feeds - files arrive constantly. Formats change without warning. Fields go missing. Values drift.

One client was processing files for months before discovering a format change had silently corrupted downstream reports. Everything below catches that at the front door.

DNA Analysis

Compares incoming files against expected patterns - field counts, distributions, formatting. Deviations flagged before anything gets processed.

Vendor data mapping

Parses third-party file structures, maps columns, flags format changes. Onboarding goes from weeks to hours.

Quality scoring

Every incoming file scored on completeness, consistency, and timeliness against historical baselines.

Requires: historical data profiles and baselines for every feed, built up as data flows through governed pipelines.
Reading and understanding documents

Make your documents searchable.

Find a specific clause across hundreds of treaty agreements, or get a plain-language breakdown of an 80-page consultation paper.

Contract clause inspection

Extracts reserving rules, schedules, and obligations from treaty documents. Built using synthetic data to test accuracy across formats and clause structures.

Enterprise knowledge search

Semantic search across internal documentation. Ask a question in plain English, get answers with source references from across the organisation.

Regulatory analysis

Structured summaries of complex regulatory guidance - what it means, what changed, what actions are required. Tested on BMA and BSCR documentation.

Spotting what is drifting

Know when assumptions stop matching reality.

Lapse rates shift. Hedge factors move. Pricing models drift gradually enough that nobody notices until the quarterly review.

01

Hedge factor tracking

Continuous monitoring against assumptions. Surfaces when lapse rates, mortality, or pricing parameters diverge from reality.

02

Anomaly detection

Adaptive monitoring that learns what normal looks like for each data stream, rather than relying on static thresholds.

03

Automated alerting

When patterns shift, the right people get context - what changed, by how much, and what it connects to.

Requires: historical baselines and time-series data stored consistently.
